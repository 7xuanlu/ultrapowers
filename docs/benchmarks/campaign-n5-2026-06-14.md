## Findings (honest)

1. **Quality: TIE.** 20/20 self-test green AND 20/20 pass the independent spec suite. No quality
   difference detected between architectures on these two tasks. Both reliably correct.

2. **Total cost (Meter B): no winner at N=5.**
   - Cost-matched pair A-sonnet vs B-parity: **$3.90 vs $4.03 median** — a dead heat (~3%, ranges
     fully overlap). Neither is cheaper.
   - Full pair A-opus vs B-full: A-opus median is lower ($4.16 vs $4.80) but its **mean is higher**
     ($5.23 vs $4.50) and its variance is much larger ($3.55–7.29 vs $3.57–5.24). Indistinguishable.
   - **No honest "X% cheaper" headline** — exactly as `bench/README.md` predicted for small N.
   - Suggestive-only (N=5, not proven): **B arms have tighter cost variance** than A-opus — the
     Workflow's deterministic orchestration may yield more predictable cost.

3. **Meter-A (coordinator context flatness): decisive — the real result.**
   - B coordinators carry **~6-7 assistant turns** (range 4–8) and **num_turns = 1**, flat across
     all 5 runs regardless of work. A coordinators carry **46–56** own turns (and the full subagent
     stream too: ~96–134 total events). **~8× flatter** coordinator context.
   - This is the orchestration moving from LLM-context (superpowers runs the loop in the main
     session) to deterministic JS (ultrapowers' Workflow runs it off-transcript on disposable
     subagents).
   - **Attribution:** this flatness is a property of **Anthropic's Workflow primitive**, inherited
     by ultrapowers — *not* an ultrapowers invention. Stated per the project's honesty rules.

## What this campaign does NOT show

- Only 2 small, single-file tasks. No hard/multi-file/coupled work where quality might diverge.
- N=5 → cost gaps are within noise; no significance testing claimed.
- **re-witness RED — the project's headline mechanism — was never exercised** (these tasks lacked
  the weak-but-passing-test failure mode it targets). Its proof remains `tests/re-witness-red/`.
- The spec suite is canonical, not adversarial/mutation-based. A stronger judge could go deeper.

## Bottom line

The benchmark delivered exactly the honest result the docs scoped: **equivalent quality, no cost
difference at small N, and a clean ~8× coordinator-context (Meter-A) flatness for ultrapowers** —
which is the Workflow primitive's property, not ours. No total-token/cost superiority claim is
warranted from this data.
